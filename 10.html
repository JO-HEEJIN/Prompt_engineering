Chapter 15: Problem Formulation

Objectives

  • To comprehend the concept of Problem Formulation within the scope of prompt engineering.
  • To differentiate between vague and well-formulated problems and understand the impact on AI's output.
  • To master the art of translating complex ideas into structured, AI-friendly prompts.
"A problem well stated is a problem half solved." — Charles Kettering

Definition

The Problem Formulation Pattern is a systematic method of translating vague ideas or needs into structured and refined prompts, ensuring efficient problem-solving and enhancing interactions with AI models.

Motivation

In the intricate dance of human-AI interaction, the precision of our prompts—the questions or tasks we pose to the AI—plays a monumental role in determining the value of output. This art and science of crafting effective prompts is termed 'prompt engineering'. However, merely understanding prompt engineering isn't enough; at its foundation lies a more fundamental process: 'Problem Formulation'.

Problem Formulation is the process of clearly defining a problem by understanding its nuances, setting specific objectives, recognizing constraints, and establishing evaluation criteria. In essence, it's about translating a vague idea or need into a structured question that AI can meaningfully address.

Applicability

  • Generative AI Interactions: Where precision in prompts is crucial for accurate outputs
  • Problem Analysis and Exploration: For thoroughly dissecting challenges
  • Delineation of Issues: When dealing with complex, interconnected problems

Structure

  1. Identification of the Problem: Recognizing the broader issue or need
  2. Gathering Relevant Information: Collecting pertinent data and context
  3. Defining the Problem Clearly: Removing ambiguities
  4. Setting Objectives or Goals: Defining desired outcomes
  5. Identifying Constraints: Recognizing limitations
  6. Establishing Evaluation Criteria: Setting success metrics

Implementation

Ultimate Problem Formulation Prompt Template

  1. Initial Exploration
    Prompt: "ChatGPT, provide an overview of [Broad Topic/Issue]."
  2. Deep Dive Research
  3. Goal Refinement
  4. Setting Criteria
  5. Understanding Limitations
  6. Iterative Exploration
  7. Feedback & Refinement

Examples

Example 1: Market Entry Strategies in Southeast Asia

Broad Topic/Issue: Expanding a business to Southeast Asian markets

Initial Exploration:
"ChatGPT, provide an overview of the current business climate and notable trends in Southeast Asian markets."

Example 2: Designing a Sustainable Urban Drainage System

Broad Topic/Issue: Urban drainage systems

Initial Exploration:
"ChatGPT, provide an overview of the challenges and requirements in designing urban drainage systems in rapidly growing cities."

Discussion

Harnessing the raw computational power of AI requires an effective method of interaction. The Problem Formulation pattern, by breaking down and structuring inquiries, can significantly enhance the user's AI engagement experience. While its merits are considerable, it's also essential to understand its challenges, limitations, and future potential.

Traditional AI models were mostly query responsive. In contrast, modern models, such as ChatGPT, provide a more dynamic, conversation-led experience. The Problem Formulation pattern bridges the gap between human curiosity and structured AI-interaction, refining broad questions into specific, targeted prompts.
